While driving, I saw a sign that said "Honk if you are a Believer!"

It got me thinking, what does it really mean to be a believer? And is that really the right label for God's people? 
Of course, those who placed the sign there had good intentions, but I couldn't help but feel that the language revealed something about a negative trend among those who claim to be Christians. 

I mean, what would really happen if I listened to the sign and honked? 
Some people would be confused, others may read the sign and recognize that I am identifying with that group, and maybe I would feel good about being public about my faith. 
Whatever the immediate reaction, that self-identification would have had no further effect on my day.
I would have gone about my day, going to the next thing, likely not thinking about the sign I had seen or what it meant.

After all, what is a believer? Someone who affirms a truth? 
-- I can say I am a believer in personal rights, and what does that require of me? 
Nothing. 
And therein lies the issue. 

That then begs the question: is that all that Christianity has become? Acknowledging that Jesus existed and claiming membership in a group that also passively affirms that? 

-- Now, we understand that few people attend Church service consistently without considering some degree of self improvement.
However, the degree to which you expect and require change varies based on conviction.
